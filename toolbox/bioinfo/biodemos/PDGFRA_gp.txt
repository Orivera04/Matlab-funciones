            Summary: This gene encodes a cell surface tyrosine kinase receptor
            for members of the platelet-derived growth factor family. These
            growth factors are mitogens for cells of mesenchymal origin. The
            identity of the growth factor bound to a receptor monomer
            determines whether the functional receptor is a homodimer or a
            heterodimer, composed of both platelet-derived growth factor
            receptor alpha and beta polypeptides. Studies in knockout mice,
            where homozygosity is lethal, indicate that the alpha form of the
            platelet-derived growth factor receptor is particularly important
            for kidney development since mice heterozygous for the receptor
            exhibit defective kidney phenotypes.
